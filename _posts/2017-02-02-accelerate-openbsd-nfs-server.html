--- layout: post title: Accelerate an OpenBSD NFS server date: '2017-02-02T11:30:00.003-05:00' author: umhau tags: - NFS - OpenBSD modified_time: '2017-02-02T11:31:20.550-05:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-2584289275272726799.post-21442072707983866 blogger_orig_url: http://nixingaround.blogspot.com/2017/02/accelerate-openbsd-nfs-server.html --- The nfs server on openbsd has been super slow.  I thought it was the result of wifi + an old computer until I tried an scp download.  the difference was several orders of magnitude.  Anyway, the solution was to cut down the size of the data packets used by the protocol.

Here's my /etc/fstab - the change is in bold.  Apparently there's a sweet spot of not-too-small and not-too-big.  These sizes are measured in bytes, by the way.  That's a packet size of 4 kb that I'm specifying.
one:/home/admin/storage     /storage  nfs  rsize=4096,wsize=4096  0 0
